What we're building

A student-founded non-profit designed to bridge between academic years, grades, and age groups through a highly accessible online database of resources.

Our purpose is to ultimately shift the focus from individual success to collective growth amongst high school students — making them capable of thriving in the navigation of high school, university, and the transition between.

How

Our main MVP is a user-friendly website organized by grade level, academics, streams, and interests.

Planned Features

01

Living Archive / Vault

A searchable database of successful college application essays, categorized by university programs and designated schools.

02

Interactive Timelines

For grades 9–12, covering major milestones and recommended steps in preparation for university — course selection, competitions, APs, IB, SAT, and more.

03

FAQ / Blog

A space where students, graduates, and alumni contribute, comment, and discuss answers to common doubts — "What I Wish I Knew" and "What I Would Have Done."

04

Monthly Interviews

A digital series featuring interviews with successful students or graduates sharing and discussing their journey.

05

Common-Goal Forums

Students tag their interests, goals, or field of study to discover peers with the same aspirations — facilitating study groups and shared advice.

06

Community Board

A shared calendar for deadlines — scholarships, competitions, sign-ups — plus a success wall for celebrating academic wins of every size.
