Grades 9–12

Interactive Timelines

Major milestones and recommended steps toward university — course selection, competitions, APs, IB, SAT, and applications — laid out grade by grade. Pick a year to see what to focus on.

  • Build strong habits

    Focus on consistent study routines and time management — the foundation everything else is built on.

  • Explore broadly

    Try clubs, sports, and activities widely. This is the year to discover interests, not narrow them.

  • Course selection

    Choose Grade 10 courses that keep options open, especially in math and science prerequisites.

  • Start a "wins" journal

    Keep a running record of projects and achievements — future-you writing essays will thank you.

  • Deepen 1–2 interests

    Begin committing to a couple of activities where you can grow into leadership by Grade 11–12.

  • First competitions

    Enter accessible contests (math, writing, science fairs) to test the waters and build confidence.

  • Consider IB / AP paths

    Learn how IB and AP work at your school and whether they fit your goals before Grade 11 selections.

  • Summer planning

    Look into volunteering, courses, or projects for the summer — unstructured months add up.

  • Grades matter most now

    Grade 11 marks weigh heavily in Canadian admissions — prioritize your core academic courses.

  • Standardized tests

    If applying to the US/UK, plan SAT/ACT timing. Register early and build a study schedule.

  • Step into leadership

    Take on a real role in your key activities — depth and initiative over a long list.

  • Research programs

    Start a shortlist of universities and programs, noting their specific requirements and deadlines.

  • Finalize your list

    Lock in target, match, and safety schools and track every deadline in one place.

  • Write your essays

    Draft early. Use the Vault for inspiration, then make it unmistakably yours.

  • Requests & references

    Ask teachers for references well ahead of time and gather any supplementary forms (AIF, portfolios).

  • Scholarships & submit

    Apply for scholarships alongside admissions — then submit early to avoid the last-minute crush.
