What I Wish I Knew

Grades open doors, but they aren't the story

I spent Grade 11 chasing a perfect average and forgot to do anything I could actually write about. Depth beats a flawless transcript.

— Alumni, UBC Science

What I Would Have Done

Start the essays in the summer

If I could redo Grade 12, I'd draft my personal profile in July. Writing under deadline in December made every sentence worse.

— Graduate, Waterloo CS

What I Wish I Knew

Ask for help earlier than feels comfortable

Teachers, older students, counsellors — everyone I eventually asked wished I'd come sooner. Nobody thinks less of you for it.

— Current student, Grade 12

What I Would Have Done

Pick two activities and go deep

A page-long activity list impressed no one. The two things I led and genuinely cared about were what people asked me about.

— Alumni, McGill Arts

Common questions

For many Canadian universities, early admission offers are based on Grade 11 and first-semester Grade 12 marks, so a strong Grade 11 year gives you the most flexibility. Grade 12 finals still matter for final/conditional offers.

Neither is universally "better." IB is a structured, broad program; AP lets you pick individual subjects. Choose based on what your school offers, your workload preferences, and the universities you're targeting.

If you're applying to US or some UK/international programs, most students take it in the spring of Grade 11 with a possible retake in early Grade 12. Canadian universities generally don't require it.

There's no magic number. Sustained commitment and leadership in a few activities almost always reads stronger than a long list of one-off memberships.
